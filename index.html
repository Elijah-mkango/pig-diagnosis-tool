CORE ADVICE FOR PIG FARMERS



BEFORE YOU BEGIN USING THIS TOOL CONSIDER THE FOLLOWING:

1. Biosecurity & Disease Prevention
  • Limit visitors and control movement of people, vehicles, and equipment into pig areas.
  • Quarantine new pigs before mixing them with the herd.
  • Regularly disinfect pens, feeding equipment, and water troughs.
  • Vaccinate against common diseases (PRRS, Mycoplasma, Erysipelas, Parvovirus, etc.) as recommended by a vet.
  • Watch for early signs of illness (fever, coughing, diarrhea, poor growth) and act quickly.

    2. Nutrition & Feeding
  • Provide a balanced diet with adequate protein, energy, vitamins, and minerals.
  • Ensure piglets receive iron supplementation to prevent anemia.
  • Avoid moldy feed to prevent mycotoxicosis.
  • Always provide clean, fresh water — dehydration worsens most diseases.

    3. Housing & Environment
  • Keep pens clean, dry, and well-ventilated to reduce respiratory diseases.
  • Avoid overcrowding — stress increases disease risk and reduces growth.
  • Maintain comfortable temperatures, especially for piglets (they chill easily).

    4. Breeding & Reproduction
  • Monitor sows for fertility, pregnancy, and farrowing health.
  • Prevent reproductive diseases (PPV, Leptospirosis, Brucellosis) through vaccination and hygiene.
  • Provide good nutrition during gestation and lactation for strong piglets.

    5. Parasite Control
  • Deworm regularly (internal parasites like roundworms, whipworms, lungworms).
  • Treat external parasites (mange, lice, ticks) with ivermectin or approved acaricides.
  • Keep bedding clean and control rodents (they spread disease).

    6. Record Keeping & Management
  • Keep records of births, deaths, treatments, vaccinations, and feed usage.
  • Track growth rates and adjust feeding programs accordingly.
  • Use records to identify recurring problems and improve herd performance.

    7. Market & Business Side
  • Stay updated on local pig prices and market demand.
  • Build relationships with reliable buyers, abattoirs, or cooperatives.
  • Explore value addition (e.g., pork processing, pig manure for fertilizer).
